    Product Management

    Products are the items you sell in your POS system. This guide explains how to create, edit, and manage products and their variants, including pricing, categorization, and stock consumption settings.

    Viewing All Products

    Navigate to the Products section from the Admin Panel sidebar to see a complete list of all products in your system.

    Product List Features

    • Product Name: The main product identifier
    • Category: Which category the product belongs to
    • Variants: Number of variants configured for the product
    • Actions: Edit and delete buttons for each product

    Products are organized by category, making it easy to find related items. You can scroll through the list or use the search function to locate specific products.

    Adding New Products

    To add a new product to your catalog:

    1. Click the Add Product button at the top of the product list
    2. Enter the Product Name (required)
    3. Select a Category from the dropdown menu (required)
    4. Add one or more Variants (see Variants section below)
    5. Click Save to create the product

    Editing Existing Products

    To modify an existing product:

    1. Locate the product in the product list
    2. Click the Edit button (pencil icon)
    3. Modify the product name or category as needed
    4. Add, edit, or remove variants
    5. Click Save to apply your changes
    Important: Editing a product affects all future transactions. Past transactions retain the product information as it was at the time of sale.

    Deleting Products

    To remove a product from your catalog:

    1. Locate the product in the product list
    2. Click the Delete button (trash icon)
    3. Confirm the deletion when prompted
    Warning: Deleting a product is permanent. All variants and their associated data (including stock consumption configurations) will be removed. This action cannot be undone.

    If a product has been used in past transactions, consider marking it as unavailable rather than deleting it to maintain historical records.

    Managing Product Variants

    Variants represent different versions or options of a product. Every product must have at least one variant to be sellable.

    What Are Variants?

    Variants allow you to offer different options for the same product. Common uses include:

    • Size Options: Small, Medium, Large
    • Flavor Options: Chocolate, Vanilla, Strawberry
    • Preparation Styles: Regular, Decaf, Extra Shot
    • Portion Sizes: Single, Double, Family Size

    Adding Variants

    When creating or editing a product, you can add variants:

    1. Click Add Variant in the product form
    2. Enter the Variant Name (e.g., "Large")
    3. Set the Price for this variant
    4. Optionally configure additional settings (see below)
    5. Repeat for additional variants

    Pricing and Tax Settings

    Each variant has its own price, allowing for flexible pricing based on size, quantity, or other factors.

    Setting Prices

    • Enter the price in your local currency
    • Prices can include decimal values (e.g., 12.99)
    • Tax is calculated based on your system settings

    Tax Behavior

    The way tax is applied depends on your Settings configuration:

    • Tax Inclusive: The price you enter includes tax; the system calculates the base price
    • Tax Exclusive: Tax is added on top of the price you enter
    • No Tax: The price you enter is the final price with no tax calculation
    Tip: Check your Settings to understand how tax affects your pricing. The same price may result in different final amounts depending on your tax mode.

    Marking Favourites

    Favourites are variants that appear in a special "Favourites" category in the POS for quick access.

    Setting a Variant as Favourite

    1. Edit the product containing the variant
    2. Find the variant you want to mark
    3. Check the Favourite checkbox
    4. Save the product

    How Favourites Work

    • Favourites appear in a dedicated "Favourites" tab in the POS
    • Multiple variants can be marked as favourites
    • Favourites from all categories are collected in one place
    • Useful for frequently ordered items or promotional products

    Customizing Button Colors

    Each variant can have a custom button color, helping staff quickly identify products in the POS grid.

    Setting Button Colors

    1. Edit the product and locate the variant
    2. Click on the Background Color picker
    3. Choose a color from the palette or enter a hex code
    4. Optionally set a Text Color for contrast
    5. Save the product

    Best Practices for Colors

    • Use consistent colors for related products
    • Ensure text is readable against the background
    • Consider color-blind users - don't rely solely on color to differentiate
    • Use high contrast for important or frequently used items

    Configuring Stock Consumption

    Stock consumption allows the system to automatically track inventory when products are sold. Each variant can be configured to consume specific stock items.

    Setting Up Stock Consumption

    1. Edit the product and locate the variant
    2. Find the Stock Consumption section
    3. Click Add Stock Item
    4. Select the stock item to consume
    5. Enter the quantity consumed per sale
    6. Repeat for additional stock items
    7. Save the product

    Example Configuration

    Coffee - Large Milk 200ml

    Note: Stock items must be created in the Stock Items section before they can be assigned to product variants.

    Category Assignment

    Every product must be assigned to a category. Categories organize products in the POS interface and determine which tills can see which products.

    Assigning a Category

    When creating or editing a product, select a category from the dropdown menu. The category determines:

    • Where the product appears in the POS category tabs
    • Which tills can access the product (based on category visibility)
    • How the product is grouped in reports

    Changing Categories

    You can move a product to a different category at any time:

    1. Edit the product
    2. Select a new category from the dropdown
    3. Save the changes
    Tip: Plan your category structure carefully. A well-organized category system makes it easier for staff to find products quickly.
