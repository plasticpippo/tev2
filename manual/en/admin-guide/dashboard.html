    Dashboard & Analytics

    The Dashboard provides a real-time overview of your business performance, while Analytics offers detailed sales reports and trends to help you make informed decisions.

    Dashboard Overview

    The Dashboard is the first screen you see when entering the Admin Panel. It provides an at-a-glance view of your current business status.

    Key Dashboard Components

    • Sales Ticker: Real-time total sales for the current business day
    • Business Day Status: Shows when the current business day started and its status
    • Till Overview: Quick view of all active tills and their status
    • Open Tabs: Summary of currently open customer tabs
    • Recent Closings: List of recent daily closing reports

    Total Sales Ticker

    The sales ticker displays the total revenue for the current business day. This figure updates in real-time as transactions are processed.

    Note: The sales total reflects all completed transactions since the business day started. It does not include open tabs that haven't been paid yet.

    Understanding the Sales Total

    • The total includes all payment methods (cash, card, etc.)
    • Tips are included in the displayed total
    • Tax is displayed according to your tax configuration settings
    • The total resets when a new business day begins

    Business Day Management

    The business day is the fundamental time period for tracking sales and generating reports. Understanding how it works is essential for accurate daily closing.

    Business Day Start

    The business day can start in two ways:

    • Automatic Start: The system automatically starts a new business day at the configured time (e.g., 6:00 AM)
    • After Manual Close: If you manually close the previous day, a new business day begins with the next transaction

    Business Day Status Display

    The dashboard shows:

    • Start Time: When the current business day began
    • Status: Whether the day is "Open" or "Closed"
    • Duration: How long the business day has been active

    Manually Closing the Business Day

    From the dashboard, you can initiate the daily closing process:

    1. Click the Close Business Day button
    2. Review the closing summary
    3. Confirm to finalize the closing
    Important: Closing the business day generates a permanent record. Make sure all transactions for the day have been processed before closing.

    Till Status Overview

    The dashboard displays the status of all configured tills in your system.

    Status Description
    Active Till is currently in use and processing transactions
    Idle Till is available but not currently processing orders
    Offline Till is not connected or has been disabled

    Each till entry shows:

    • Till name
    • Current status
    • Device assignment (if applicable)
    • Today's transaction count

    Open Tabs Summary

    The Open Tabs section shows all customer tabs that have not yet been closed. This helps you track outstanding balances.

    For each open tab, you can see:

    • Tab name or identifier
    • Current balance
    • Number of items
    • Time the tab was opened
    • Assigned table (if applicable)
    Tip: Regularly review open tabs to ensure customers haven't forgotten to settle their bills.

    Date Range Selection

    Analytics reports can be filtered by date range to focus on specific periods.

    Available Date Filters

    • Today: Current business day only
    • Yesterday: Previous business day
    • Last 7 Days: Rolling 7-day period
    • Last 30 Days: Rolling 30-day period
    • Custom Range: Select specific start and end dates

    Hourly Sales Chart

    When viewing "Today" in Analytics, you'll see an hourly sales breakdown. This chart helps identify peak business hours.

    Using the Hourly Chart

    • Each bar represents one hour of operation
    • Height indicates total sales for that hour
    • Hover over bars to see exact values
    • Use this data to optimize staffing schedules

    Sales Trend Charts

    For historical date ranges, Analytics displays trend charts showing how sales have changed over time.

    Trend Chart Features

    • Line Graph: Shows daily sales totals over the selected period
    • Comparison: Compare current period with previous periods
    • Average Line: See your average daily sales
    • Peak Indicators: Highlight best-performing days

    Exporting Reports

    Analytics data can be exported for further analysis in spreadsheet applications or accounting software.

    Export Options

    • Print: Generate a printer-friendly version of the current report
    • PDF: Save the report as a PDF document
    • CSV: Export raw data for spreadsheet analysis
    Tip: Export reports regularly to maintain historical records beyond what's stored in the system.
