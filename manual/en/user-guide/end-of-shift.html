    End of Shift

    Learn how to properly log out, understand session persistence, and hand over to the next staff member.

    Logging Out

    To log out of the POS system:

    1. Locate your username in the order panel (right side of the screen)
    2. Click the Logout button next to your name
    3. You will be returned to the login screen

    Understanding Session Persistence

    Important information about how data persists in the system:

    What Persists After Logout

    • Open Tabs - All saved tabs remain in the system
    • Table Status - Current table assignments remain
    • Order History - All completed transactions are saved

    What Does NOT Persist

    • Current Order - Unsaved orders are lost when you log out
    • Login State - The next user must log in with their credentials
    Tip
    Before logging out, check if there are any open tabs that need to be processed. Saved tabs will be available for the next staff member to access.

    End of Shift Checklist

    Before ending your shift, complete these steps:

    1. Process Pending Orders

    • Check for any open tabs
    • Process payments for orders that are ready
    • Transfer items to other tabs if needed
    • Close empty tabs

    2. Verify Table Status

    • Check that tables are properly marked
    • Ensure completed orders are paid

    3. Complete Logout

    • Click Logout in the order panel
    • Confirm logout if prompted
    • Wait for the login screen to appear

    Handing Over to Next Staff

    When handing over to the next staff member:

    1. Process or transfer open tabs - Don't leave active orders unattended
    2. Inform the next staff - Tell them about any pending orders
    3. Log out completely - Don't leave your session active
    4. Hand over verbally - Briefly explain any ongoing situations
    Best Practice
    A good handoff includes: open tab names, table status, and any special instructions. This ensures continuity of service for customers.

    Daily Closing (Admin)

    At the end of the business day, administrators should perform a Daily Closing:

    1. Ensure all transactions are complete
    2. Click the Admin button
    3. Go to Daily Closing
    4. Review the closing summary
    5. Click Close Day to finalize
    Note
    Daily closing is typically done by a manager or supervisor at the end of the business day. It generates reports and resets certain daily counters.

    Reports Overview

    Administrators can access various reports:

    • Transaction History - View all completed sales
    • Daily Closing Summary - End-of-day financial report
    • Analytics - Sales trends and performance metrics
    • Itemised Consumption - Stock usage by product

    These reports help track business performance and can be accessed from the Admin Panel.

    Security Reminder

    Remember these security practices at the end of every shift:

    • Always log out - never leave your session active
    • Don't share passwords - each user has their own account
    • Secure the terminal - ensure no one else can access it
    • Report issues - tell your administrator about any concerns
